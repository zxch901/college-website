Northwest Mississippi Community College

Degree: The Associate of Applied Science in Graphic Design Technology.

Years Needed: 2+ years

Tuition: This can range anywhere from $5,000 to $16,000 depending on if you are in-state or out of state.

The Graphic Designer combines concepts and facts from the humanities, social sciences and technology while utilizing critical design methodoligies in visual problem solving. Upon successful completion of this 64 hour program, an Associate of Applied Science degree is awarded.

Graphic artists need a bachelor's degree in graphic design, web design, art, animation, or a related field. You'll need highly developed computer skills, with a mastery of the Adobe suite of products, such as Photoshop, InDesign, and Illustrator.

This is designed to provide a solid foundation in technical skills and techniques combined with a design theory in the area of graphic design. Provide a broad understanding of related disciplines including arts, humanities, and technology. Provide extended experience with hands on training in screen printing, vinyl sign making, and laser engraving and cutting.

This prepares you for success in the graphic design industry. Learn from industry proffessionals in small class sizes, on- campus or online. You'll master the principles and technical skills required to combine visual designs, color theory and the fundamentals of composition to create digital or print projects. Graduate with confidence and a portfolio to launch your dream career as a graphic designer.

Your portfolio is king. Make it practical. Show design process for some projects if possible. Showcase your work. If you are housing your portfolio on a website, make sure that website is perfectly designed. Always be prepared and ready for anything!9
